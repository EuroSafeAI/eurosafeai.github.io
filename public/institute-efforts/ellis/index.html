What this shows

A regional comparison of top-tier ML research output — not a safety analysis. Every accepted paper at ICLR/ICML/NeurIPS 2025–2026 counts, regardless of topic, because ELLIS is a general AI-research network. The narrative mirrors comparing economies (Europe vs US vs China).

Data sources (all primary)

  • Papers & counts: OpenReview API — every accepted paper per venue (Poster/Spotlight/Oral).
  • Affiliations: parsed from the first page of each paper’s arXiv PDF (title→arXiv via Semantic Scholar), on a stratified random sample of all accepted papers (equal draw per venue).

How regions are assigned

A paper is credited to every region that appears among its author affiliations (institutions + country/state tokens), so an international collaboration counts for each partner (participation). For the 100%-stacked composition, each paper is split equally across the distinct regions present (attributed share). Regions: USChina (incl. Hong Kong)Europe (geographic + UK/CH)CanadaOther / rest of world. Precision-first matching, guarded against look-alikes (US state “Georgia” vs the country; Cambridge MA vs UK; Microsoft Research Asia is in Beijing; Israel counted as Other, not Europe).

Known limitations

  • Affiliation figures are a sample with a margin, and only cover papers with a retrievable arXiv full text — a lower bound. Papers whose affiliations we could not identify are shown as Unknown and excluded from the attributed shares.
  • Region assignment is any-author, so participation rates overlap (sum > 100%); the attributed shares are the ones that sum to 100%.
  • Total accepted counts ARE exhaustive; only the regional split is sampled.
